About Dataset

This dataset contains team, player, and match data for European Professional Soccer from seasons 2008-2016. There are 7 datasets in total that each relate with a key, either country, match, team id, etc. The datasets included are Country, League, Match, Player, Player_attributes, Team, and Team_attributes.

For the team and player data, there are columns of quantitative and qualitative data. Some examples of the qualitative data are included in player_attributes and team_attributes such as a players preferred_foot, attacking_work_rate and defensive_work_rate along with a teams buildUpPlaySpeedClass, chanceCreationPassingClass and others.

Initial Questions:

  • What are the attributes of winning teams?
  • What are the qualities for specific players that contribute to a team’s win?
  • How can you visualize a whole season?
  • How big was the margin of a win?
  • What, from a data perspective, does a large margin win look like vs. a small margin win?
  • How do the in-game stats compare for each match?

    Nodal Graph

    As seen in the image below, we had the idea to create nodes that will represent a match. You start the visualization by filtering for league, season, and two teams. We did this filtering because the graph would be too large and busy to be well understood. We played around with it but felt these filters gave us the best and most meaningful visual.

    Each node is a match shown by the two participating team logos. Every node is at least one of the two teams filtered at the beginning. This creates a web for all the games played by two teams in one league in a single season. We created a yellow box around the node for when the two teams selected play each other. The arrows between the two teams represent a match up. The arrows are color coordinated to the match outcome; blue is a win, red is a loss, and gray is a tie.

    The nodal graph answers the question of how to visualize an entire season. This graph can also answer the question of if momentum could affect match outcomes.

    Drill Down Graphs

    Our goal is to add additional drill down or attribute items to the nodal graph. Within each match of the overall graph, we wanted to create hover over abilities to show key indicators (comparing team attributes), players who made large impacts, and additional important factors of the game.

    The first drill down graph is a radar graph designed to show the comparison between the two teams in a match. The graph allows for a person to have a visual representation of the team's differences in their style of play. This allows us to answer the question of what are the attributes of a winning team.

    The different types of play include:

  • buildUpPlaySpeed
  • buildUpPlayPassing
  • chanceCreationPassing
  • chanceCreationShooting
  • chanceCreationCrossing
  • defencePressure
  • defenceAggression
  • defenceTeamWidth

    • These attributes have a numeric value that is given in the original dataset. We created a function that takes in the match id based on which node you click on in our original node graph.

    The next drill down graph shown below the radar graph is a comparison of the two best players on the participating teams, determined by which players had the highest overall rating scores for that match. With the given player attribute data, we decided that, besides the overall rating, the attributes that contribute to a highly rated player include acceleration, vision, jumping, and stamina. These attributes are gathered from the date of the match (as the dataset contains attribute data for players over time) and compared visually with a bar chart. They are split by team, so the home team is on the left and away team is on the right. Comparing each team's top player performance provides some additional insight to a match. This graph appears along with the radar graph when a match is clicked on. We decided on the colors taking into consideration colorblindness. We had started with blue, orange, green, red, and purple but decided the blue, maroon, green, yellow, and dark blue was easier to distinguish across all types of colorblindness.

    The last drill down graph is actually two visuals. Along with the two visuals previously mentioned, when you click on a specific match on the node graph, you will see a bidirectional bar chart on the right. This bar chart shows the in-game statistics from the selected match. The in-game statistics include:

    • Goals
    • Shots on Goal
    • Shots off Goal
    • Possessions
    • Crosses
    • Corner Kicks
    • Penalties
    • Cards (Yellow and Red)

    These in-game stats allow us to see the specifics of the match and another aspect of team play that contributes to a win or loss. Another aspect of this visual is that when you double click on a certain bar of this chart you will see when that team previous has that exact stat in another game and whether they won, loss or tied. For example, when you double click on the bar of Leicester City's "crosses", it will show other games where this team has 13 crosses and what the outcome was of that game. The goal of that interaction is to show if that statistic, whether high or low, had an impact on the outcome of the game.

    Color Choices

    When deciding on colors, we wanted to select symbolic and stereotypical colors for the nodes, lines, and charts. We also wanted to pick colors that will be visible for those users that are color blind or have color impaired vision. We used the color blindness simulator, Color Oracle to test if the colors I chose would be sufficient with “Deuteranopia”, “Protanopia”, and “Tritanopia” color vision. Color Oracle is a color tool recommended by Dr. Reda.
